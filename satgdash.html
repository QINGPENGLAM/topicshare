DASHES

  1. To indicate non-essential statements within a sentence (Grammatically, dashes are identical to commas when used this way.)

    London – which is a very old city – has many new buildings.
  2. Before a list, an explanation, or to create a deliberate pause in a sentence(Grammatically, dashes are identical to colons when used this way)

    London has many tourist attractions – museums, palaces, and monuments.
