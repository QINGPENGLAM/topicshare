Definition

  • gross domestic product (GDP)-measure of the final output of a nation’s economy. GDP measures the total value of all new goods and services produced in an economy in a given year.
  • income approach to GDP- adding up all of the income earned in country in certain period. Such as adds up wages, rents, interest, and profits.
  • expenditures approach to GDP-all spending on final goods and services in an economy. such as consumption, investment, government spending, exports, and imports
  • value-added approach to GDP-adding up all of the value added at various stages of production
  • final goods and services-the goods and services that are purchased by consumers, businesses, the government, or other countries in their final form for their intended final use(goods)
  • intermediate goods-goods that are used in the production of a final product
  • consumption (C)-spending by households on final goods and services in a given year but excludes spending on new housing
  • investment (I)spending businesses do in order to produce goods and services (such as buy computers for accountants to use or build factories to build cars)( real assets—the spending on physical equipment, research and development, and inventory.)
  • government spending (G)-spending by government entities, whether local or national governments, on goods and services such as building roads and national defense; note that transfer payments are not included in “government spending”
  • transfer payment-any payment by a government to a household that is not in exchange for a good or service
  • exports (X)-goods that are produced in one country and then sold within another country
  • imports (M)-goods that are produced in a different country than where they were purchased
  • net exports X-M-spending on exports minus spending on imports’ “exports” is the value of goods that go out of a country, “imports” is the value of the goods that come into a country
