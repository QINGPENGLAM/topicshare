Derivative of inverse function ,inverse trignometry and logrituem

  • d/dx of inverse function is the reciprocal of d/dx of original function at inverse point

Explain

  • Y2 is the inverse of Y1
  • The d/dx of Y2(inverse function) will be 1/(d/dx Y1)

Note

  • d/dx of trig can be written as sin^-1(x) or arcsin(x)
  • Just take a look of the first three

Derivative of ln and e

  • d/dx (lnU) = 1/U *U'
  • d/dx (e^x) = e^x
