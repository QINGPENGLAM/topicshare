Complex Number

Complex number is combination of a real number and imaginary number
Form: a+ib
where a = real number and ib = imaginary number
a,b belongs to real numbers and i = √-1.

Side note

  • Real number is everything except √ (negative number)
    Example: √-2, √-7, √-11 are all imaginary numbers
  • Zero is a real number
  • Conjugate -changing the sign between the two terms in the denominator value
  • FOIL method

FaQ

  1. How to divide complex number?
    You multiply by its conjugate then apply the FOIL method to simplify the expression.
  2. What is the additive identity and inverse of complex numbers?
    additive identity of complex numbers:(x+yi) + (0+0i) = x+yi
    additive identity : 0+0i.
    additive inverse of complex numbers is written as (x+yi)+ (-x-yi) = (0+0i)
    additive inverse : -x-yi.
  3. What is the multiplicative identity and inverse of the complex number?
    multiplicative identity of complex numbers is defined as (x+yi)
    (1+0i) = x+yi
    the multiplicative identity : 1+0i
    multiplicative identity of complex numbersis defined as (x+yi)
    (1/x+yi) = 1
    the multiplicative identity : 1/x+yi.
