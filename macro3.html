Limitations of GDP

  • Gross national product (GNP)-what is produced domestically and what is produced by domestic labor and business abroad in a year. (production of citizens and firms of a country)
  • National income-all income earned: wages, profits, rent, and profit income.
  • Net national product (NNP)-GNP minus depreciation (national income, which includes all income to businesses and individuals and personal income, which includes only income to people)
  • Depreciation-process by which capital ages over time and therefore loses its value

Well being society

  • GDP is an indicator of a society’s standard of living
  • standard of living is all elements that affect people’s happiness, whether these elements are bought and sold in the market or not

What not count toward GDP

  • Non marketing service
  • Black market
  • Non report tax job

Not gather as GDP but affect well being

  • pollution
  • Health
  • Stress
  • Community
  • Illegal action
