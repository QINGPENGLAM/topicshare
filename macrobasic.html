Factor that affect the demand

  • Expectation of futre price If you expect the price will go up in the future you'll rather buy it now than buy it later so the demand will increase
  • People's income You have more money means you are able to buy more the demand increase (This only works for normal good)For example when you buy a car if you have money you obviously going to buy a better care including factor like safety, or trying to brag about it
  • Increase of population More people = more demand
  • People's preference If a author is on a popular show and advertised it then people assume this item is better
Demand(Whole curve) =all of the quantities of a good or service that buyers would be willing and able to buy at all possible prices Inferior good = a good for which demand will decrease when buyers’ incomes increase. substitute good(other related product) = goods that can replace each other; when the price of a good increases, the demand for its substitute will increase.(Price of related product up demand up. Price down demand down. Because compare to other your product is more worth) Demand curve= a graph that plots out the demand schedule, which shows the relationship between price and quantity demanded
Quantity Demand(at a point) = the specific amount that buyers are willing to purchase at a given price; each point on a demand curve is associated with a specific quantity demanded.(Price up QD down , Price down QD up) Normal good = a good for which demand will increase when buyers’ incomes increase. Complement good(Need to have both) = goods that tend to be consumed together; when the price of a good increases the demand for its complement will decrease.(Price up demand down, price down demand up. Because a ebook and tablet, tablet price up there less money to buy ebook if afford tablet) Demand schedule = a table describing all of the quantities of a good or service (It can be used to make a demand curve)

Supply

  • supply(similar to demand) = a schedule or a curve describing all the possible quantities that sellers are willing and able to produce, at all possible prices they might encounter in a particular period of time
  • The law of supply = positive relationship between price and quantity supplied, leading to an upward-sloping supply curve
  • quantity supplied = the amount of a good or service that sellers are willing to sell at a specific price
  • Change in quantity demand = change along the curve
  • Change in supply= change the curve left and right

Factor that affect the supply

  • the number of sellers in a market more people selling = more supply there is
  • the level of technology used in a good’s productionbetter technology= lower input and easier to get the supply
  • the prices of inputs used to produce a goodinput up supply down, input down supply up.(less input means you have more profit)
  • the amount of government regulation, subsidies or taxes in a marketregulation up supply down because less profit
  • the price of other goods sellers could produceOther goods can earn more then seller would rather plants more profit goods
  • the expectations among producers of future pricesSeller know the goods will increase future so supply safe till later to earn more

Market

  • Market equilibrium

    • Terms

  • market- interaction of buyers and sellers where goods, services, or resources are exchanged
  • shortage- more demand than supply
  • surplus- more supply than demand
  • equilibrium- occurs when price has adjusted until quantity supplied is equal to quantity demanded (When there no shortage or surplus/ intersect point on graph)
  • disequilibrium- when an shortage or surplus occur theres disequilibrium
  • equilibrium price-“market clearing price” the price of the item at equilibrium status
  • equilibrium quantity- the quantity that will be sold and purchased at the equilibrium price
