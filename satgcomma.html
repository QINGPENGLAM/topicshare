COMMAS (DO)

  1. Before a coordinating conjunction to join two full sentences
    (FANBOYS: For, And, Nor, But, Or, Yet, So)
  2. Between a dependent clause and an independent clause when the dependent clause comes first
    (begin with subordinating conjunctions such as because, when, until, unless, since, before, and after.)
  3. Around non-essential words and phrases
    (When information between the commas is crossed out, the sentence still makes grammatical sense)
  4. To separate items in a list
  5. To separate adjectives whose order could be reversed

COMMAS(NOT DO)

  1. Between two full sentences (independent clauses)
  2. comma + pronoun (it, they, s/he, one, you, I)
  3. Between two clauses with the same subject when the subject is not repeated
  4. Between Subjects and Verbs
  5. Between Compound Elements (Nouns, verbs, adjectives, etc. linked by and)
  6. efore or After a Preposition
    (of, to, by, from, about, with, in, on, at)
  7. Before or after the word “that”
  8. Between two adjectives whose order cannot be reversed
  9. Between adjectives and nouns
  10. Before an open parenthesis
