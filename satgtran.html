Transitions

  • Questions testing transitions are more about meaning than grammar
  • Whenever you encounter a transition question, cross out the transition and state the relationship between the sentences/clauses (similar ideas/contrasting ideas) before you look at the answer choices.

Example

Incorrect: The obvious reason for the construction of the Great Wall of China was protection, since China had no powerful enemies at the time construction first began.

(The two halves of the sentence express opposing ideas)
Correct: The obvious reason for the construction of the Great Wall of China was protection, but China had no powerful enemies at the time construction first began.

Important

  • If there is an OMIT/DELETE option, or an option without a transition, check it FIRST because it will usually be correct

  • When two transitions have the same meaning (e.g. but/yet) and are both grammatically acceptable in context, both can automatically be eliminated because no question can have more than one right answer.
